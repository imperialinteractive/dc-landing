The Mechanisms

Why Mobility = Watts

Three biomechanical pathways — each one measurable, each one addressable with the right protocol.

01
Hip Extension Efficiency

Restricted hip flexors reduce your power stroke length. When your hip can't fully extend on the downstroke, you're leaving raw watts on the table with every single pedal revolution. More range of motion = more work done per stroke = more watts for the same effort.

02
Aerodynamic Position

Tight hamstrings and lower back prevent you from holding an aggressive, low position without compensating through your lumbar spine. Fix the flexibility, and you can maintain the aero tuck — the same position that pros spend $8,000 on equipment to simulate.

03
Fatigue Resistance

Muscle imbalances force compensating muscles to work harder than they should. Your back is doing work your glutes should be doing. Your hip flexors are doing what your core should manage. This burns matches you'd otherwise have for the final climb.
